Introduction

A place to begin

Renewable energy is energy that is derived from abundant and continuously replenished natural resources, such as wind power, solar power, geothermal power, hydropower, power derived from biomass, and nuclear power.

Benefits of renewable energy for the environment include reducing greenhouse gas emissions and some types of air pollution from generating power using fossil fuels as well as allowing for more diverse options when it comes to selecting the kind of energy used, thus reducing dependence on imported, environmentally unfriendly fuels.

In the long term, renewable energy does not incur fuel costs once installed, and they generally have lower operation and maintenance costs. There are also green loans that can be provided for environmentally friendly initiatives as well as tax credits and incentives specifically for renewable energy.
